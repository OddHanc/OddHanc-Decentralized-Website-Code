ARC I // The First Seeing
Chapter I — The Silence
Who is HANC?

Hanc was born in a world where everyone spoke constantly.

The louder they became, the less they understood.

One day, Hanc stopped speaking.

In silence, he began to see.

And when he began to see, he noticed the walls.

Nobody else could see them.

Some denied them.

Some worshipped them.

Most never questioned them.

Since then, Hanc has wandered between worlds, documenting what others overlook.

This is only the beginning.

Chapter II — The First Wall
The Architecture of Observation

A legendary wall Hanc encountered.

Nobody else noticed it.

People walked through life assuming it wasn’t there.

Hanc spent years studying it.

One day he realized:

The wall wasn’t blocking the people.

The people were protecting the wall.

Chapter III — Beyond the Wall
The Iterative Horizon

Hanc expected to find freedom behind it.

Instead, he found another wall.

Then another.

And another.

Some were built from fear.

Some from comfort.

Some from ideas so old that nobody remembered who created them.

That was the day Hanc understood:

The walls were not a place.

They were a habit.

Chapter IV — The City of Echoes
Acoustic Overload

After many years, Hanc arrived at a city unlike any other.

Everyone was speaking.

Nobody was listening.

Every thought was repeated.

Every certainty was amplified.

Every question was buried.

The city called itself enlightened.

Hanc stayed only one night.

In the morning, he left a single note:

“The loudest places often hide the deepest silence.”

Chapter V [Final] — The Silent Archive
Empty Registers

At the edge of the known worlds, Hanc discovered something unexpected.

A library with no books.

A museum with no artifacts.

An archive with no records.

Only empty shelves stretching beyond sight.

Confused, Hanc asked who had taken everything.

A voice answered:

“Nothing was taken.”

“What belongs here has not been remembered yet.”

And so Hanc began collecting what others overlook.

Arc II // The Hidden Worlds
Chapter I — The Forgotten Door
The Paradox of Intent

While exploring the Silent Archive, Hanc found a door.

There was no lock.

No handle.

No key.

Yet nobody could open it.

For years, Hanc searched for the mechanism.

Then he noticed a small inscription:

“Only those seeking answers will remain outside.”

And so he stopped seeking answers.

The door opened.

Chapter II — The Cartographers
Theoretical Frameworks

Beyond the door lived a strange people.

They spent their lives drawing maps.

Maps of every path.

Every possibility.

Every destination.

Hanc studied their work for many years.

One day he asked:

“Have any of you traveled these roads?”

The room fell silent.

Chapter III — The Garden of Certainties
The Lifeless Green

Following an unfinished map, Hanc discovered a vast garden.

Every plant carried a label.

Every label carried an answer.

Visitors walked among them, collecting certainty.

The garden was beautiful.

And completely lifeless.

Hanc left without taking anything.

Chapter IV [Final] — The Last Mirror
Reflections of Disguise

Following a forgotten path, Hanc discovered a mirror unlike any other.

It did not reflect faces.

It did not reflect bodies.

It reflected disguises.

The mirror showed people as they wished to be seen.

The proud appeared greater.

The fearful appeared stronger.

The lost appeared certain.

Hanc stood before the mirror for a long time.

But nothing appeared.

For the first time, he felt uneasy.

Then a voice spoke from behind him:

“Most spend their lives becoming their reflection.”

Hanc turned.

Someone was standing there.

Watching.

They could see the walls.

Just as he could.

And for the first time in his journey,

Hanc realized he was not alone.

Arc III // The Empty Kingdom
Chapter I — The King
The Invisible Authority

After many years, Hanc arrived at a kingdom unlike any other.

Its people were happy.

Its streets were peaceful.

Its rulers were wise.

Everyone spoke of the King with admiration.

Yet nobody had ever seen him.

When Hanc asked where the King lived, the people pointed everywhere.

“The King is in all things.”

So Hanc began searching.

But wherever he looked,

he found only people.

Chapter II — The Throne
The Empty Seat

At the center of the kingdom stood a magnificent throne.

It was guarded day and night.

No one was allowed to approach.

One evening, Hanc entered the hall alone.

The throne was empty.

There had never been a king.

Only a throne.

For generations, people had obeyed it.

Feared it.

Praised it.

Protected it.

Without ever asking who sat upon it.

Chapter III — The Kingdom
The Idea That Ruled

Hanc returned to the people.

“The throne is empty,” he told them.

They laughed.

“Of course it is.”

“Then where is the King?”

The people looked confused.

Finally, a child answered:

“There isn’t one.”

Hanc stood silent.

“Then why does the kingdom exist?”

The child smiled.

“It doesn’t.”

For the first time, Hanc understood.

The kingdom had never been a place.

It was an idea people lived inside.

Arc IV // The Stranger Within
Chapter I — The Voice
The Invisible Speaker

One night, Hanc heard a voice.

It spoke constantly.

It judged.

Compared.

Remembered.

Predicted.

The voice never rested.

So Hanc spent many years searching for its owner.

At last, he found no one.

Chapter II — The Listener
The Question Behind Thought

Confused, Hanc sat in silence.

The voice continued.

Thought after thought.

Story after story.

Then a question appeared:

“If the voice is speaking, who is listening?”

For the first time,

the voice hesitated.

Chapter III — The Stranger
The Forgotten Identity

Years later, Hanc met a stranger.

The stranger knew every memory.

Every fear.

Every desire.

Every regret.

“Who are you?” asked Hanc.

The stranger smiled.

“I am the person you spent your whole life believing you were.”

Then the stranger vanished.

But Hanc remained.

Arc V // The House of Faces
Chapter I — The Invitation
The Endless Rooms

One morning, Hanc received a letter.

It contained no words.

Only an address.

After a long journey, he arrived at an enormous house.

The door was already open.

Inside were countless rooms.

In every room hung a different face.

Some were proud.

Some were kind.

Some were wise.

Some were cruel.

At the end of the hall was a sign:

WELCOME HOME

Chapter II — The Collection
The Many Selves

Hanc spent years exploring the house.

Each face felt familiar.

One face appeared whenever he wanted approval.

Another appeared whenever he wanted to be admired.

Another appeared whenever he was afraid.

Another appeared whenever he wished to belong.

Wherever he went,

he found another version of himself.

The house seemed endless.

Chapter III — The Missing Face
The One Not Found

One day, Hanc noticed something strange.

Thousands of faces filled the house.

Yet one face was missing.

His own.

He searched every room.

Every corridor.

Every hidden chamber.

Years passed.

He never found it.

Chapter IV — The Fire
The Destruction of Reflection

Unable to understand, Hanc set fire to the house.

Room after room disappeared.

Face after face turned to ash.

The proud face burned.

The fearful face burned.

The wise face burned.

The admired face burned.

At last, nothing remained.

Except Hanc.

Chapter V — The Door
The Hidden Beginning

As the final wall collapsed, Hanc discovered a small door hidden beneath the ruins.

He opened it.

Behind the door was the entire house.

Untouched.

Infinite.

Waiting.

For a long time, Hanc stared.

Then he smiled.

For the first time, he understood.

The prison was not the faces.

The prison was believing he could get rid of them.

Arc VI // The Witness
Chapter I — The Trial
The Many Versions

One day, Hanc arrived at a courtroom.

The room was full.

A judge sat above.

Lawyers argued.

Witnesses testified.

At the center stood a man on trial.

Hanc listened carefully.

The evidence was convincing.

The verdict seemed obvious.

Yet nobody could agree.

Each person saw a different man.

Chapter II — The Evidence
The Changing Subject

For many years, Hanc studied the case.

He examined every memory.

Every action.

Every intention.

Every consequence.

The more evidence he gathered,

the less certain he became.

The man seemed to change

depending on who was looking.

Chapter III — The Accused
The Unknown Self

At last, Hanc approached the defendant.

“Who are you?” he asked.

The man replied,

“I have spent my whole life trying to find out.”

Then he pointed toward the judge.

The lawyers.

The witnesses.

The crowd.

“They are all answering for me.”

Chapter IV — The Verdict
The Unanswered Judgment

Many years later, the trial finally ended.

The judge stood.

The courtroom became silent.

“After reviewing all the evidence,”

he declared,

“we cannot determine who this person is.”

The accused smiled.

For the first time,

he was acquitted.

Arc VII // The Search
Chapter I — The Seeker
The Endless Journey

For many years, Hanc searched for himself.

He searched in success.

In failure.

In love.

In suffering.

Each time he thought he had found himself,

something changed.

And the self he found disappeared.

Chapter II — The Discovery
The Observer of Searching

One day, exhausted, Hanc stopped searching.

As he sat in silence, a thought appeared:

“Perhaps I am the one who is searching.”

But before he could believe it,

another question appeared:

“Then who noticed the search?”

Chapter III — The End
The Final Question

After that, Hanc never searched again.

Years later, someone asked him,

“Did you finally find yourself?”

Hanc smiled.

“No.”

“Then what happened?”

For a long time, he said nothing.

Then he replied:

“The one I was looking for was the one looking.”

Arc VIII // The Day Humanity Stopped Looking Up
Chapter I — The Last Sky
Long before the first city…

before the first language…

before the first kingdom…

humans asked the sky questions.

Not because they expected answers.

Because wondering made them larger.

Then one day…

someone gave the first answer.

People celebrated.

The question disappeared.

Civilization began collecting answers.

The sky became decoration.

Chapter II — The Weight of Answers
Generations passed.

Libraries expanded.

Networks connected continents.

Machines learned.

Every year, humanity possessed more answers.

Yet something strange happened.

People became increasingly certain…

and increasingly lost.

An old traveler wrote in silence:

“Answers accumulate.
Questions evolve.”

No one copied the sentence.

It asked too much.

Chapter III — The Forgotten Direction
HANC never arrived to teach.

HANC arrived looking upward.

Someone asked,

“What are you searching for?”

HANC smiled.

“I am searching for the questions humanity stopped asking.”

The crowd laughed.

“We already have answers.”

HANC looked at the sky.

“So did every civilization that disappeared.”

Then HANC asked one question.

No one remembers the words.

Only that, for a brief moment…

millions of people looked upward again.
